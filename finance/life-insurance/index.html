The DIME Method

DIME is the most comprehensive way to estimate life insurance needs. It accounts for four key areas:

  • D — Debt: All non-mortgage debts your family would need to pay off
  • I — Income: Years of income replacement until dependents are self-sufficient
  • M — Mortgage: Full mortgage payoff so your family keeps the home
  • E — Education: College costs for each child

The 10× Rule (Quick Estimate)

A simpler rule of thumb: get 10× your annual income in coverage. This is fast but less accurate than DIME — it doesn't account for your actual debts, number of children, or existing assets.

Term vs. Whole Life Insurance

  • Term life: Coverage for a set period (10, 20, 30 years). Cheapest option. Most financial advisors recommend this for pure protection.
  • Whole life: Permanent coverage with a cash value component. Much more expensive. Only consider if you've maxed out all other tax-advantaged accounts.

When Do You Need Life Insurance?

If others depend on your income — spouse, children, aging parents — you likely need life insurance. If you're single with no dependents and significant assets, you may need little or none.
