Margin vs. Markup — what's the difference?

Margin is profit as a percentage of revenue: (Revenue − Cost) / Revenue.
Markup is profit as a percentage of cost: (Revenue − Cost) / Cost.

A 50% markup gives you a 33% margin. A 100% markup gives you a 50% margin. They're measuring the same profit — just against different denominators. Always clarify which one you're using.
