What This Means

The True Cost of Employment

For every dollar you pay an employee in salary, you typically spend an additional $0.20–$0.40 on taxes and benefits. The "employee multiplier" — total cost divided by base salary — usually falls between 1.25 and 1.45 for US employers. Larger companies with richer benefits often run 1.5–1.8x. This calculator reflects mandatory federal taxes (FICA + FUTA) plus optional state unemployment (SUTA), benefits, and overhead you configure. Workers' compensation and state-specific taxes vary widely — check your state's requirements for precision. The recruiting one-time cost should be amortized over expected tenure when comparing options.
