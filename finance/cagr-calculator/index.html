CAGR smooths out year-to-year volatility to give you the steady annual rate that would get you from your starting value to your ending value. It does not reflect what happened in each individual year — it's the geometric mean return.

What is CAGR? Compound Annual Growth Rate is the rate at which an investment would have grown if it grew at a steady rate each year. It's the standard way to compare investments of different lengths. A stock that went 0%, +100%, +0% has a CAGR far lower than one that returned 26% each year — even though the first tripled in value over three years.
