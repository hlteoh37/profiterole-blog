What is Yield to Maturity (YTM)?

  • YTM is the total annualized return if you buy the bond today and hold it until maturity, assuming all coupon payments are reinvested at the same rate.
  • Current yield = annual coupon / current price. Simpler but ignores capital gain/loss at maturity.
  • Premium bond: Price > Face value. This happens when the coupon rate > market rates. YTM < coupon rate.
  • Discount bond: Price < Face value. Coupon rate < market rates. YTM > coupon rate.
  • Par bond: Price = Face value. YTM = coupon rate.

Example: US Treasury 10-Year Note

  • Face value: $1,000 | Coupon rate: 4.5% | Years: 10 | Semi-annual coupons
  • If trading at $950 (discount) → YTM ≈ 5.08% (higher than coupon because you gain $50 at maturity)
  • If trading at $1,050 (premium) → YTM ≈ 3.96% (lower than coupon because you lose $50 at maturity)
  • Corporate bonds add a credit spread on top of the equivalent Treasury YTM
